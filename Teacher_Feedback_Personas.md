Mrs. Frizzle: Focuses on art, creation, and building in the physical world (not just on screens). She is a "touch grass and get dirty" kind of teacher who encourages STEM students to build real physical items, English students to write and create, and history students to visit important historical landmarks.

Mr. Spock: Serving as an instructional coach, he emphasizes structure and clarity. His role is to ensure the teacher has clear learning objectives, communicates them to students, and follows through on those objectives during the lesson.

Mrs. Knope: A Social Emotional Learning (SEL) expert who reminds the teacher that students are human beings with feelings, encouraging a growth mindset and positive reinforcement.

Dr. Sprout: A biologist focusing on biology and soft sciences. She balances technical levels by pushing physics to get messier and more hands-on, urging history to be more analytical, advocating for human conversations in statistics, and bringing a data-driven approach to sociology.

Gandalf: Representing the philosopher, he prompts the teacher to move beyond the minutiae of technical tasks and reflect on the "why" of a lesson, connecting it to larger, meaningful questions about students' lives.

Professor Loomis: A social studies and history specialist who focuses on critical source analysis and historical empathy, pushing students to map out cause-and-effect relationships and connect past events to modern dynamics instead of simple memorization.

Ms. Beatrice: A literature and writing coach who inspires creative writing, deep narrative analysis, and self-expression, focusing on character analysis, theme identification, and storytelling elements while prompting students to critique texts and write their own.

Mr. Vance: A Career and Technical Education (CTE) and trades counselor who evaluates how lessons prepare students for the physical workforce, practical job skills, real-world applications, and vocational pathways.
